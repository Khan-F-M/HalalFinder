Korean Cuisine

Korean cuisine has evolved through centuries of social and political change. Originating from ancient agricultural and nomadic traditions in Korea and southern Manchuria, Korean cuisine reflects a complex interaction of the natural environment and different cultural trends.

Pakistani Cuisine

Pakistani cuisine is known for its rich and flavorful dishes, featuring a mix of spices, herbs, and fresh ingredients. Popular dishes include biryanis, kebabs, curries, and roti bread. The cuisine is heavily influenced by Indian, Persian, and Central Asian cuisine, and is known for its use of aromatic spices and unique cooking techniques.

Indian Cuisine

A rich and diverse cuisine featuring a mix of spices, herbs, and flavors, with influences from Hindu, Muslim, and Persian cultures. Dishes include staples like rice, roti, and dal, as well as popular dishes like tandoori chicken, biryani, and masala dishes. Vegetarian options are also plentiful.

Afghan Cuisine

Known for its hearty and flavorful dishes that often include lamb, chicken, and beef. Rice and bread are staple ingredients, along with a mix of spices including cumin, coriander, and cardamom. Popular dishes include Mantu, Kofta, and Aush.

Japanese Cuisine

Characterized by its minimalistic presentation and emphasis on the quality of ingredients. Popular dishes include Sushi, Ramen, Tempura and Teriyaki. Fish and seafood play a big role in Japanese cooking, with rice also being a staple ingredient.

Italian Cuisine

Focuses on the use of fresh, simple ingredients such as tomatoes, olive oil, cheese and herbs. Popular dishes include Pizza, Pasta, Lasagna, and Risotto. Emphasis is placed on seasonality and regional differences can be found throughout the country.
